New York



New York is the biggest city in the U.S.A. It has about 14 million inhabitants. The density is one of the highest in the world, about 9,000 people to 1 sq km. It is sometimes called "The Big Apple”. New York has 5 major parts (districts): Manhattan, Queens, the Bronx, Staten Island and Brooklyn. Originally New York was called New Amsterdam, because its first inhabitants were the Dutch. It was a small town situated on Manhattan Island and surrounded by a Wall against Indians. The wall of course doesn't exist now, there is a famous Wall Street in its place. Wall Street is known as the centre of financial life in the U.S.A., with the most important stock - exchange in the world. Wall Street is situated in the south of Manhattan, while black quarter Harlem is in the northern part of Manhattan. Manhattan City Plan New York was built on a modern plan of streets and avenues which are numbered. Streets run east-west and avenues north-south (e.g. Fifth Avenue is the shopping centre) Only few of them have got names e. g. Wall Street or Broadway, which runs nearly the whole length of the island. Broadway is now a famous theatre and cultural centre.
